Hi, I'm Chiaki. Welcome to my portfolio!

I'm a graduate student at UW Tacoma studying Master's of Science in Geospatial Technologies set to graduate in August of 2020. I previously graduated from UW Seattle in Spring 2020 with a Bachelor of Arts in Geography.

I have a wide range of interests, including map design, web design and mobile app development, as well as visual design and art. My motto is to create maps that reflect socioeconomic issues that are not only easy to understand but also pleasing to look at.

Work Highlights

Below are some of my most notable projects. Clicking an item will take you to the website or dedicated page for that project.

Racial and Social Equity in Seattle

Designed a website featuring interactive equity maps created with Mapbox. These maps were designed to represent spatial analysis of racial and socioeconomic Seattle census tract data.

A History of the Music Scene in Capitol Hill

Designed a self guided tour to showcase the history of Capitol Hill's music scene using WordPress. The theme of this page was inspired by the iconic rainbow crosswalks of the neighborhood.

Transit Accessibility in Low-Income Neighborhoods

Designed an informative map poster showing public transit accessibility in low income census tracts. Used ArcGIS to develop a Transit Accessibility Factor and generate maps to reflect which areas received the most frequent and proportional service.

Income and Medical Access in King County

Designed a website featuring interactive choropleth maps created with Leaflet.js. This map shows the relationship between median income and medical facility access based on King County census data.
